Below is a list of the beers we plan to be rotating on our taps.

Anticipation

Anticipation

IPA

Who doesn't wait in anticipation for good things to happen? Stanley and Leroy mostly anticipate breakfast and napping, but we were anticipating brewing a good IPA and we think this makes the cut. India Pale Ales or IPAs are known for their bold, strongly hopped flavor. Our version has just that, with a slightly bitter profile and a balancing malt background.

Bad Bad Leroy Brown

Bad Bad Leroy Brown

American Brown Ale

An American Brown Ale of Course! This dark brown beer has a slightly sweet toffee-like flavor, is mildly hopped and is a great beer for those a bit hesitant to try a dark beer as it does not have the typical roasted bitter taste of most porters and stouts. Give it a try!

Bassito

Bassito

Hefeweizen

A HEFEWEIZEN is an effervescent medicaments bodied Bavarian-style wheat beer that was traditionally consumed on Sunday morning after (and perhaps before and during) church. The top fermenting yeast makes this very flavorful and a great beer to enjoy with a friend. HEF's can be delicious with a bit of fruit added in the secondary fermentation. Check the board to see which variation is on tap.

Belter

Belter

Scottish Ale

When we brewed our first Scottish Ale, our tasters kept belting them down and commented that the taste was excellent so we knew it would be a keeper that had to have the perfect name. It was destiny that when looking up Scottish slang we found the word "belter" is to be excellent, great, fantastic. This ale has a deep color with rich, smoky, malty flavors and aromas. If only we could get the bassets in a kilt!

Bottoms Up

Bottoms Up

Vanilla Porter

This is a dark beer made with a unique blend of chocolate, caramel, aromatic and black malts, with vanilla beans added in the scondary fermentation. It is delicious. We are aging part of the batch in bourbon barrels from the Stonehouse Distillery and expect that to be ready early 2017.

Breaking Basset

Breaking Basset

Farmhouse Ale

This beer represents what we are all about. Being located in a beautiful ranching and farming community it is only fitting that we serve a Farmhouse Ale. This beer is based on the simple, rustic ales which were brewed to refresh, sustain and comfort a hard-working body and mind.

Chasing Blonde

Chasing Blonde

Blonde Ale

Blonde ales are a modern beer popularized in the 1990's as a way to introduce American consumers (who were used to traditional mass-market beers) to the wonders and diversification of craft beers. This light-bodied ale is crisp and well balanced with a slightly malty taste. Some of our tasters can't stop chasing blondes...

Digger

Digger

Dunkleweizen

This Dunkelweizen is a darker version of a Hefeweizen. Made with wheat and other complex malts this brown, unfiltered beer can have pronounced vanilla, banana, apple and clove flavors. If you give it a try we're certain you'll dig it!

Drooligan

Drooligan

Irish Red Ale

Who can resist a beer with a name like this? It is our version of an Irish Red Ale. A refreshing beer with an amber hue that comes from roasted barley. The flavor is slightly sweet with a hint of caramel, lightly hopped and finishes clean and smooth. One of our favorites!

Extra Special Basset

Extra Special Basset

Extra Special Bitter

An ESB is a British style beer with a rich, coppery color. It is brewed with a complex mix of low alpha acid hops. The beer is malty and bitter without the bite. Stanley and Leroy are really chuffed about this brew; they think it's a real corker!

Honey I'm Home

Honey I'm Home

Honey Amber Ale

Whether a visitor to Meagher County or one of the colorful locals, this beer is sure to make you feel at home. This Honey Amber Ale is infused with Montana-Made honey, resulting in a beer with a beautiful hue and aromas of malt, fruit and honey, finishing with a delightful sweetness.

Stanley Stout

Stanley Stout

Milk Stout

Don't let the name fool you! Stouts are often mistaken as dark, heavy beers with high ABVs, but many such as Stanley Stout are highly drinkable. This Milk Stout is a full-bodied beer with a creamy sweetness that counters the roasted character. This is a rich and complex beer that is quite satisfying.

Whistle Wetter

Whistle Wetter

Belgian Witbier

This brew is fashioned after a Belgian Witbier or "white beer." It is light and delicious with herbal tones and a lemony finish that is the perfect for the summer months. Top it off with an orange wedge and enjoy!
